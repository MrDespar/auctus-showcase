# Frameworks Reference

## 2x2 Matrix: Common Axis Pairs by Industry

*Technology/SaaS:* Product breadth × Customer segment, Integration depth × Geographic reach

*Consumer/Retail:* Price point × Product range, Online × Offline presence

*Financial Services:* Product complexity × Customer sophistication, Scale × Specialization

*Healthcare:* Care setting × Payer mix, Technology enablement × Service breadth

*Industrial:* Customization × Scale, Geographic scope × Vertical focus

## AUCTUS DACH Mid-Market Extensions

*B2B Services (HVAC, FM, Industrial Maintenance):*
  - Service breadth × Geographic density (DACH coverage depth)
  - Recurring revenue % × Customer concentration

*Software / Vertical SaaS:*
  - Sector specialization × Customer size (SMB vs. enterprise)
  - On-premise legacy × Cloud-native (migration risk as competitive moat)

*Healthcare Services / Medical Devices:*
  - Reimbursement dependency × Private-pay mix
  - Geographic density × Payer diversification

*Business Process Outsourcing:*
  - Automation level × Labor intensity
  - Vertical focus × Service breadth

## Buy-and-Build Lens: Fragmentation Indicators

A sector is considered "highly fragmented" (buy-and-build attractive) when:
- Top 5 players have < 40% combined market share
- Revenue of #1 player < 5× revenue of #10 player
- No dominant standard or platform creates lock-in at scale
- Customer relationships are local/regional (geographic fragmentation)
